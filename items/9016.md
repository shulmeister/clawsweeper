## Summary

Keep open. Current main still provides estimated/local cost reporting and downstream transcript aggregation, but it does not capture OpenRouter's provider-reported `usage.cost` / `usage.cost_details` from streaming responses or expose that exact per-message cost through agent-runtime metadata. The ClawRouter comment is a useful external workaround, and #7006/#9696 are related, but neither supersedes this provider-reported OpenRouter cost request.

## Best Possible Solution

Keep #9016 open. The right fix is to capture OpenRouter `usage.cost` and `usage.cost_details` at the OpenAI-compatible streaming transport boundary, persist the provider-reported cost in normalized assistant usage/transcripts without replacing existing estimate paths, and expose it through a structured agent-runtime surface such as `session_status` details or a dedicated usage/cost tool. The Telegram footer can then render exact provider cost when available and fall back to local estimates otherwise.
