## Summary

Keep open. Current `main` does not implement the requested exec-specific critical escalation, so this is not `implemented_on_main`; but the PR as proposed is not a safe cleanup close either, because it promotes `exec` to a hard block using `recentCount` across the whole history window rather than the consecutive/no-progress evidence that every existing critical detector uses.

## Risks / Open Questions

- The underlying gap may still be real: on current `main`, repeated `exec` calls do not escalate at `criticalThreshold` unless they also satisfy a separate no-progress/global-breaker path.
- The PR's approach appears over-broad: because `recentCount` counts matching `exec` calls anywhere in the retained history, it can hard-block legitimate reused commands that are interleaved with other work or producing changing results.
