## Summary

Current `main` already addresses the reported zero-promotion behavior. Deep ranking now gates on total accumulated signal (`recallCount + dailyCount + groundedCount`) and context diversity across queries/days, so `recallCount: 0` and a low single-hit `maxScore` no longer force zero candidates. The reporter’s follow-up also matches current behavior, and the same implementation is present in release `v2026.4.23`.

## Evidence

- **Deep ranking no longer depends on raw recallCount alone:** `totalSignalCountForEntry` sums `recallCount`, `dailyCount`, and `groundedCount`, and `rankShortTermPromotionCandidates` uses that combined `signalCount` plus `max(queryHashes.length, recallDays.length)` for the deep gates.
  - file: [extensions/memory-core/src/short-term-promotion.ts:390](https://github.com/openclaw/openclaw/blob/a9797214338ba31c52c796adbb75afb16e0684a9/extensions/memory-core/src/short-term-promotion.ts#L390)
  - sha: [a9797214338b](https://github.com/openclaw/openclaw/commit/a9797214338ba31c52c796adbb75afb16e0684a9)
- **Deep gate logic on shipped release:** The deep pass checks `signalCount < minRecallCount` and `contextDiversity < minUniqueQueries`, rather than requiring raw `recallCount` and raw `queryHashes.length` to hit the thresholds independently.
  - file: [extensions/memory-core/src/short-term-promotion.ts:1211](https://github.com/openclaw/openclaw/blob/a9797214338ba31c52c796adbb75afb16e0684a9/extensions/memory-core/src/short-term-promotion.ts#L1211)

  - file: [extensions/memory-core/src/short-term-promotion.test.ts:474](https://github.com/openclaw/openclaw/blob/a9797214338ba31c52c796adbb75afb16e0684a9/extensions/memory-core/src/short-term-promotion.test.ts#L474)
