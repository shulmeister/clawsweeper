## Summary

Current `main` contains a targeted WhatsApp fix for reconnect-driven pairing replies: historical DMs are suppressed during reconnect windows, the reconnect timestamp is wired into inbound processing, and regression tests cover the exact historical-vs-live pairing behavior.

## Evidence

- **Reconnect guard in WhatsApp access control:** `checkInboundAccessControl` computes a pairing-reply grace window and suppresses pairing replies when a DM timestamp predates the current socket connection by more than 30s, preventing historical messages from triggering new pairing codes during reconnect cycles.
  - file: [extensions/whatsapp/src/inbound/access-control.ts:57](https://github.com/openclaw/openclaw/blob/a79c40a789c31f07408be59b5fbdee5c8a916eec/extensions/whatsapp/src/inbound/access-control.ts#L57)

  - sha: [a79c40a789c3](https://github.com/openclaw/openclaw/commit/a79c40a789c31f07408be59b5fbdee5c8a916eec)
- **Reconnect timestamp is passed from the inbox monitor:** The WhatsApp inbox monitor records `connectedAtMs`, derives `messageTimestampMs` from each inbound WhatsApp message, and passes both into `checkInboundAccessControl`, so the historical-message suppression is active on the live reconnect path.
  - file: [extensions/whatsapp/src/inbound/monitor.ts:109](https://github.com/openclaw/openclaw/blob/a79c40a789c31f07408be59b5fbdee5c8a916eec/extensions/whatsapp/src/inbound/monitor.ts#L109)
