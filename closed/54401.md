## Summary

Current main and the shipped release line already harden the MEDIA/local attachment path against the reported host-file disclosure class. Local media hydration is constrained to allowed roots, host-read/root expansion is tied to the effective filesystem/read policy, and sender-scoped group policy denial prevents a group sender from gaining host media read capability. The remaining GHSA notification concern is a security-process/admin matter rather than an OpenClaw source change.

## Best Possible Solution

Keep the shipped MEDIA/local attachment hardening and handle any remaining GHSA notification or advisory-status concern through the private security/advisory process, not as an open source-repo code/docs issue.
